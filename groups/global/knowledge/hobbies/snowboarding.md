## Board Research (Mar 2026)

Researched all-mountain twin/directional twin boards for switch practice on groomers. Key requirements: wide (US 12), 159-160W, camber-dominant, medium-stiff flex.

**Top candidates (2025/2026 model year):**
1. **Capita Mercury 160W** (265mm waist) — camber-dominant hybrid, medium-stiff, directional twin. Perfect size match. No Channel mount (use strap bindings). ~$900-950 NZD.
2. **Burton Custom Camber 158W** (262mm waist) — traditional camber, medium-stiff, directional twin. Best edge hold, Channel compatible (EST Step On). No 159/160W available. ~$700-750 NZD.
3. **Jones Mountain Twin 159W** (261mm waist) — CamRock hybrid, medium flex, directional twin. Traction Tech 3.0 serrated edges excellent for NZ ice. No Channel mount. ~$850 NZD.
4. **Ride Algorhythm 160W** (264mm waist) — standard camber, medium flex, directional twin. Good groomer board. No Channel mount. ~$800 NZD.
5. **GNU Riders Choice 158W** (268mm waist) — C2X hybrid (rocker-dominant), medium-stiff, asym twin. Magne-Traction edges. Interesting for switch (asym sidecut). ~$650 NZD.

**Eliminated:** Burton Process (too soft), Salomon Assassin (sizing gap), Never Summer Proto T3 (sizing gap), YES Typo (discontinued), Korua (no wide twins), Lib Tech Skate Banana (wrong camber profile).

**Decision factors:** Mercury wins on size match and camber profile. Custom wins on Channel compatibility and pure edge hold. Mountain Twin wins on edge tech for NZ conditions.

### Extended Brand Research (Mar 2026)

Researched Lib Tech, Bataleon, Rome, and Ride lineups for true twin / directional twin boards with wide sizes, camber-dominant profiles, medium-stiff flex for switch practice on groomers.

**Lib Tech:**
- **T.Rice Pro** — true twin, C2 (camber-rocker-camber) + Magne-Traction, flex 6/10, wide: 157W/161W/164W. No 159-160W. ~$910 NZD at BaseNZ (sale). Good switch board but sizing gap.
- TRS — discontinued for 2025-2026 season. Was a strong candidate (true twin, C3, 157W/159W).
- Cold Brew — directional (not twin), eliminated.
- Skate Banana — rocker-dominant, soft flex, eliminated (again).

**Bataleon (3BT Triple Base Technology):**
- **Whatever 159W** (271mm waist) — true twin contact, medium camber + 3BT, flex ~5-6/10. Most forgiving for switch learning due to 3BT catch-free design. Very wide. ~$1,080 NZD at NZ Shred.
- **Goliath+ 158W** (266mm waist) — directional twin, medium camber + 3BT, flex 7/10. Stiffer/more aggressive than Whatever. Tri-ax laminate, 7000 sintered base. ~$1,150 NZD at NZ Shred.
- Global Warmer — flex 3/10 park jib board, eliminated.

**Rome:**
- **Warden 158W/161W** — directional twin, Fusion Camber, flex 5-6/10. Budget option (~$590 NZD). Extruded base is a downgrade.
- Ravine — directional (tapered, asymmetric camber), eliminated.
- National — discontinued, replaced by ReneGade.

**Ride:**
- **Algorhythm 160W** (264mm waist) — directional twin, standard camber, flex ~5-6/10. Already on shortlist. ~$999-1,100 NZD.
- Shadowban 160W — directional twin, mellow camber, flex ~4-5/10. Too soft. ~$1,100 NZD.
- Berzerker — directional (not twin), flex 8/10 freeride board, eliminated.

**Note on Bataleon 3BT:** The uplifted base edges near contact points reduce edge catch dramatically, which is specifically beneficial for switch learning. Tradeoff is slightly less raw edge hold on hardpack compared to flat-base camber boards. SideKick tech adds hull-shaped nose/tail for float and smooth edge-to-edge transitions.
