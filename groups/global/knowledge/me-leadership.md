# Nathan Donaldson — Personality & Leadership Profile

## Overview

Nathan is a strategic, future-oriented leader who thrives on generating original ideas, evaluating them through intuition and discernment, and inspiring others to pursue what's possible. He is a voracious learner who actively shares frameworks and knowledge with his team. He guides rather than runs his company (Boost), operating as a visionary catalyst rather than an operational executor.

## Assessment Summary

### DISC: DC Profile
High Dominance and Conscientiousness with some Influence. Drives quick decision-making and systematic research. Friction with repetitive admin tasks.

### CliftonStrengths (Full 34)
**Top 5:** Futuristic, Individualization, Relator, Ideation, Strategic
**6–10:** Learner, Communication, Activator, Empathy, Achiever
**Bottom 5:** Discipline, Context, Harmony, Consistency, Includer

Leads with **Strategic Thinking** themes. Executing themes are notably weak — he is wired for vision and ideation, not for systematic follow-through or maintaining consistent processes.

### Working Genius (Lencioni)
- **Genius** (natural, energising): **Invention** (creating original ideas) + **Discernment** (intuitive evaluation of ideas/plans)
- **Competency** (capable, neutral): **Wonder** (pondering possibility) + **Galvanizing** (rallying people to action)
- **Frustration** (draining): **Tenacity** (pushing tasks to completion) + **Enablement** (ongoing encouragement/support on others' projects)

### Holding Space 360° (Nowhere Group, 2021)
A facilitative leadership assessment based on feedback from 4 direct reports and 4 colleagues.

**Strongest quadrants:** Opening Space (inspiring others, challenging status quo, using questions) and Catalysing Breakthrough (seeing unseen paths, using constraints for innovation, leading people to surprise themselves).

**Being Spectrums** — Nathan consistently sits on the "Holds Space" side:
- Sees opportunities (not obstacles)
- Inspires (not demands)
- Puts people at ease (not on edge)
- Likes to learn (not to know)
- Pushes people to surprise themselves (not work harder)
- Holds a quiet centre under pressure
- Uses multiple speeds and ways of knowing

High self-awareness (small gap between self and others' ratings). High consistency across raters.

## Consistent Themes Across All Assessments

**Superpower pattern:** Nathan generates ideas, evaluates them intuitively, sees the future, and inspires others to pursue it. He creates the conditions for breakthroughs rather than executing them himself.

**Key strengths:**
- Voracious, active learner who shares knowledge freely
- Future-oriented strategic vision — always asking "what's possible?"
- Brings multiple perspectives and reframes to problems
- Inspirational communication that enrolls people in a compelling story
- Creates psychologically safe spaces for honest, challenging conversations
- Guides rather than directs — holds space for others to grow

**Consistent growth edges:**
- Under stress, reverts to solving/providing answers and can become abrupt (catching people off guard)
- Low energy for follow-through, sustained execution, and administrative tasks
- Can be distant from the wider team — strong connection with leadership tier but less visible to the broader organisation
- Tends to move to the next interesting idea before fully landing current priorities
- Assumes others share his appetite for creative tension/conflict
- Likes to challenge the status quo but can resist when his own status quo is challenged

## How to Work With Nathan

- Lead with possibility and ideas — he engages deeply with "what if" and novel framing
- Don't expect him to drive operational follow-through; pair him with executors
- Give him space to think and explore before converging on solutions
- Be direct and concise — he values speed and dislikes fluff
- Challenge his thinking — he respects it, even if his initial instinct is to push back
- If he's stressed, he may jump straight to solutions; slow him down with questions
- He values learning and will engage with new frameworks, books, and perspectives
- He prefers to guide from behind rather than command from the front
